Addressing the concerns

Three questions answered.

🔒
“Will companies share their data?”
DimeTech doesn’t access customer data. We only verify the donation flow — transaction total in, charity receipt out. No names, no addresses, no payment details.
💰
“Is Uber really saving, or just claiming what they collected?”
With the price-first structure, the 11¢ is Uber’s revenue. Uber donates its own money. If it were a pass-through, Uber gets $0. The structure is everything.
🚫
“People hate fees. This will incense them.”
It’s not a fee. Not a surcharge. Not a separate line on the receipt. It’s built into the price. The customer sees $18.84. The story is “Uber donates from every order” — positive, not extractive. Canadian consumer law requires surcharge disclosure — this avoids that entirely.
